Description

Koeksisters are a traditional South African dessert made from fried dough that is soaked in a sweet, sticky syrup. They are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, with a deliciously sweet flavor that is perfect for a special treat or celebration.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (50g) sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup (60g) butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (240ml) milk
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Syrup:

  • 2 cups (400g) sugar
  • 1 cup (240ml) water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 cinnamon stick or 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. Make the Dough:
    1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
    2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, eggs, and milk.
    3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until a soft dough forms.
    4. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to 1/4 inch (6mm) thickness.
    6. Cut the dough into strips about 4 inches (10cm) long and 1/2 inch (1cm) wide, then twist each strip into a braid.
    7. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (180°C).
    8. Fry the koeksisters in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
    9. Remove the koeksisters from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  2. Make the Syrup:
    1. In a saucepan, combine the sugar, water, cream of tartar, lemon juice, and cinnamon.
    2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
    3. Remove the cinnamon stick if used, and let the syrup cool slightly.
    4. Dip the warm koeksisters into the syrup, coating them completely.
    5. Allow the koeksisters to soak in the syrup for at least 1 hour before serving.
