Melktert Recipe

Home Melktert Bunny Chow Koeksisters

Description

Melktert (Milk Tart) is a traditional South African dessert, with a smooth, custard-like filling in a crisp, buttery pastry crust. The filling is usually flavored with cinnamon and vanilla, giving it a warm, comforting taste.

Ingredients

Pastry Crust:

,
  • 1 1/2 cups (180g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (50g) sugar
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1–2 tablespoons cold water (if needed)
  • Pinch of salt

For the Filling:

  • 4 cups (1 liter) whole milk
  • 1/2 cup (100g) sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup (30g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cinnamon stick or 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • Ground cinnamon for dusting

Steps

  1. Make the Pastry Crust:
    1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
    2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt.
    3. Add the cold, cubed butter and rub it into the flour mixture with your fingertips until it resembles breadcrumbs.
    4. Add the egg yolk and mix until the dough comes together. If the dough is too dry, add 1–2 tablespoons of cold water.
    5. Press the dough into a greased 9-inch (23cm) tart pan, covering the bottom and sides evenly.
    6. Prick the base with a fork and bake for 15–20 minutes, or until lightly golden. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  2. Make the Filling:
    1. In a saucepan, heat the milk and cinnamon stick (if using) until just below boiling. Remove from the heat and let it cool slightly.
    2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, eggs, flour, cornstarch, and salt until smooth.
    3. Slowly pour the warm milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to prevent curdling.
    4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens to a custard consistency.
    5. Remove from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Discard the cinnamon stick if used.
    6. Pour the custard into the baked pastry crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
    7. Allow the tart to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set.
    8. Before serving, dust the top with ground cinnamon.
